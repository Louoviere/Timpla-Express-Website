Our Specialties Menu

Chicken Adobo

Chicken Adobo is a customer favorite, featuring tender chicken marinated in a flavorful blend of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices. Slow-cooked to perfection, this dish captures the essence of Filipino comfort food with its irresistible combination of tanginess and richness. Served with steamed rice, it's a heartwarming meal that's sure to bring joy to your table.

Carbonara

Carbonara is a timeless favorite, boasting creamy pasta tossed with crispy bacon, Parmesan cheese, and a hint of black pepper. With each bite, you'll experience the comforting flavors of Italian cuisine and the creamy richness that makes Carbonara irresistible. Whether enjoyed as a quick weeknight dinner or a cozy weekend indulgence, Carbonara never fails to satisfy cravings and warm hearts with its simple yet comforting charm.

Our Meals Menu

Tasty Lumpia

Lumpia is a spring roll filled with a mixture of minced meat (usually pork or chicken), finely chopped vegetables like carrots, cabbage, and sometimes bean sprouts. It's seasoned with soy sauce and other spices, then wrapped in a thin crepe-like wrapper and fried until crispy. Tasty lumpia often includes a sweet and tangy dipping sauce made from vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and sugar.

Pancit Bihon

Pancit Bihon is a noodle dish made with thin rice noodles stir-fried with vegetables, meat (often chicken, pork, or shrimp), and sometimes sliced sausage or liver. The dish is seasoned with soy sauce, fish sauce, and citrus juice like calamansi or lemon. its delicious flavor and symbolism of long life.

Balbacua

Balbacua is a hearty stew made from slow-cooked beef or pork skin, tendons, and other cuts of meat. It's simmered for hours until the meat is tender and infused with rich flavors from spices like star anise, garlic, and black pepper. Balbacua is often served with rice and enjoyed with condiments like soy sauce, vinegar, and chili for added flavor.

Chicken Pochero

Pochero its hearty and flavorful broth. Chicken Pochero typically includes chicken pieces simmered with vegetables such as cabbage, potatoes, carrots, and plantains (saba bananas), along with tomatoes, onions, and garlic. It's seasoned with bay leaves, salt, and pepper, resulting in a comforting and satisfying dish often served with rice.

Linarang

Linarang is a stew made with fresh fish, cooked in a sour broth flavored with vinegar, ginger, tomatoes, and green chilies. It's a tangy and savory dish with a hint of spiciness from the chilies, balanced by the acidity of the vinegar. Linarang is typically served hot and enjoyed with rice.
